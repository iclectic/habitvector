# HabitVector Adaptive Lab — Product Vision

## The Problem

Conventional habit trackers are built around a single model: a daily checklist, a streak counter, and a chart.
This model works well for people with highly consistent schedules. It fails — and actively harms motivation — for people whose days change.

Shift workers, carers, healthcare workers, students, parents, freelancers, and contractors do not have the same morning routine every day. They do not have the same energy, the same available time, or the same cognitive load. When a standard habit tracker shows a broken streak on a night-shift day, it communicates failure where none actually exists.

## The Vision

HabitVector Adaptive Lab is a privacy-first, explainable habit experimentation platform that:

1. **Learns which conditions make a habit achievable** for each individual.
2. **Suggests the right version of a habit** (minimum, standard, or stretch) based on context.
3. **Explains every recommendation** in plain language.
4. **Lets users run structured personal experiments** — testing whether a morning versus evening schedule, a five-minute versus twenty-minute version, or a cue-paired versus uncued approach actually changes their completion rate.
5. **Celebrates recovery** as much as uninterrupted streaks.
6. **Operates entirely locally by default** — no data leaves the device without explicit consent.

## Who It Is For

- People with rotating or irregular shift patterns
- Carers who cannot predict their daily availability
- Students with variable academic schedules
- Parents whose routines depend on others
- Contractors and freelancers with changing workloads
- Anyone whose life does not fit a fixed daily template

## What Makes It Different

| Standard Tracker | HabitVector Adaptive Lab |
|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Checklist + streak | Context-aware suggestion + explanation |
| One version per habit | Minimum, standard, and stretch versions |
| Fixed reminders | Shift-aware, adaptive reminder timing |
| Punishment for missed days | Recovery intelligence + "never miss twice" guidance |
| Charts of raw data | Friction Map — patterns by day, shift, energy |
| No experiments | N-of-1 personal experiment engine |
| Opaque algorithm | Fully explainable, auditable recommendations |
| Data leaves device silently | Explicit consent required for every external action |

## Definition of Success

A user with an irregular schedule understands — without guessing — why they complete a habit on some days and not others, and has been given practical, evidence-based suggestions for how to change that pattern. They were not shamed for missing days. They made a real, measurable change.
